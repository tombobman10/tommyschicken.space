breeds

At first, I bought 5 road island reds. after that, I bought a mixed set, so I have no clue what they are. we do, however, know what 11 hens are. they are isa browns. these are very great birds.

housing

I originally built a chicken coop for my few starter hens. but when I needed to upgrade, I had to buy a second coop. I considered expanding the original, but I then realized that the hens would fight the new hens. so I built a second coop and connected the 2 with a run.

about me

because I love talking about myself this section is quite long. I am Tommy #### (keeping the last name secret for privacy). I started raising hens very recently. I realized that I could take their eggs and sell them. eventually, I was making enough money to cover the cost. I was profit positive. it didn't stop there, I had an appetite for money. I took all the money I had, from doing chores and other stuff, and threw it all together to get a few things. I got 20 hens, a chicken coop, and their starter food. when they ate their food I moved them out of the incubator and into the hen house. and now, only now, are the new girls starting to lay.

feed

I feed my hens only the finest stuff in the world (which is why their eggs taste so good). they get weat, oats, and corn. along with compostable foods, they also roam the yard and eat random insects that they find. giving the hens freedom, statistically, makes them happier. and happy hens make happy eggs, so you should buy them.
